Adsum IoT Coder covers the embedded inner loop across IoT silicon — scaffold, build, flash, test, observe, and fix. It ships today for Nordic nRF and Espressif ESP32, and expands by adding chip families, protocols, and workflows as self-contained modules rather than full rewrites.

## What ships today and what is next

| Category | Shipping today | Next |
|:---|:---|:---|
| **Platforms** | Nordic nRF, Espressif ESP32 | More vendors as the community asks |
| **SoC families** | nRF52, nRF53, nRF54 · ESP32, ESP32-S3, ESP32-C6 | nRF7x (Wi-Fi), nRF9x (cellular), more ESP32 variants |
| **Protocols** | BLE, Wi-Fi | Thread, Matter, LTE-M |
| **Workflows** | Prototype, build/flash/debug, add a feature, test & validate, log capture and analysis | Power-budget and protocol-correctness review |
| **Hardware-in-the-loop** | RTT/UART and serial-log capture, J-Link multi-device control | BLE sniffer, power profiling, spectrum analysis |
| **Benchmark** | v0.1 — 6 BLE tasks on nRF | v0.2 — 20+ tasks, ESP suite, more comparisons |

CRA-readiness workflows for the EU Cyber Resilience Act are planned as a separate release. The roadmap is shaped by what the community asks for and contributes.

<Tip>
  Have a chip family, protocol, or workflow you need? [Open an issue or propose a benchmark task](https://github.com/adsumnetworks/Adsum-IoT-Coder/issues) on GitHub — the roadmap is driven by community feedback.
</Tip>

## Limitations

We publish what is true today, not what we wish were true.

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Scope">
    Today the extension ships for Nordic nRF52/53/54 (BLE) and Espressif ESP32/S3/C6 (Wi-Fi, BLE). Other Nordic families (nRF7x, nRF9x) and other protocols (Thread, Matter, LTE-M) are on the roadmap, not yet shipping.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Benchmark sample size">
    Six tasks is a proof of concept, not statistical significance. Benchmark v0.2 targets 20 to 30 tasks and includes an ESP suite alongside expanded nRF coverage.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="SDK coverage">
    All benchmark tasks ran on a single NCS version (v3.2.1). Support for older LTS SDK versions is a roadmap item.
  </Accordion>
